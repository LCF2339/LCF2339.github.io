1. What's the difference between a griddle and a grill pan?

Answer: Griddles are flat cooking surfaces ideal for pancakes and eggs, providing even heat distribution. Grill pans, with raised ridges, impart grill marks and smoky flavors to your food, simulating outdoor grilling.

2. Can I use a griddle on any cooktop?

Answer: Most griddles are versatile and compatible with various cooktops, including gas, electric, and induction. Always check the manufacturer's guidelines to ensure compatibility with your specific cookware.

3. How do I clean a grill pan with raised ridges?

Answer: Cleaning a grill pan is easy. Allow it to cool, then use a brush or sponge to scrub between the ridges. For stubborn residue, soak the pan in warm, soapy water before cleaning.

4. Are grill pans suitable for all types of meat?

Answer: Yes, grill pans are versatile and work well with a variety of meats, including steaks, chicken, and even vegetables. The raised ridges create beautiful grill marks and enhance the flavors of your favorite dishes.
